US-CRM-001 - DUNEX Multi-Division CRM Scaffold

As a DUNEX operations leader,
I want a Salesforce CRM foundation organized by business division,
so that Studio, Academy, Events, and Publishing Kiosk teams can manage their work in dedicated apps with consistent records, navigation, and access control.

Background:
The implementation establishes four Lightning apps named DUNEX_Studio, DUNEX_Academy, DUNEX_Events, and DUNEX_Publishing_Kiosk. It creates eight custom objects: Studio_Project__c, Shoot_Schedule__c, Academy_Course__c, Academy_Enrollment__c, Event_Service__c, Event_Booking__c, Publication__c, and Distribution_Channel__c. It also creates custom tabs, page layouts, Lightning record pages, and app home pages for those records and grants access through the DUNEX_CRM_Access permission set.

Acceptance criteria:
1. The DUNEX_Studio, DUNEX_Academy, DUNEX_Events, and DUNEX_Publishing_Kiosk apps are available in the scratch org.
2. The Studio_Project__c and Shoot_Schedule__c objects support Studio operations.
3. The Academy_Course__c and Academy_Enrollment__c objects support Academy operations.
4. The Event_Service__c and Event_Booking__c objects support Events operations.
5. The Publication__c and Distribution_Channel__c objects support Publishing Kiosk operations.
6. Each division has dedicated page layouts and Lightning record pages for its custom objects.
7. Each Lightning app has a dedicated home page for administrator-led testing.
8. DUNEX_CRM_Access provides visibility to the apps, tabs, and objects required for unit testing.
9. The metadata deploys successfully to dunex-scratch-1.

Implementation notes:
The scaffold now includes the first experience layer with layouts and Lightning pages, and it remains the baseline for later automation, reports, dashboards, and sample data.
